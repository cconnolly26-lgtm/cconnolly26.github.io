Team History

The Golden State Warriors were founded in 1946 as the Philadelphia Warriors, one of the original teams of the Basketball Association of America (BAA), which later became the NBA.

In 1962, the franchise moved to California and became the San Francisco Warriors. In 1971, they changed their name to the Golden State Warriors to represent the entire state. The team played many years in Oakland before moving to the state-of-the-art Chase Center in San Francisco for the 2019–20 season.

Championships

The Warriors have won 7 NBA championships:

  • 1947 & 1956 (Philadelphia era)
  • 1975 (Rick Barry-led upset over the Washington Bullets)
  • 2015, 2017, 2018, 2022 (the Stephen Curry era)

Fun Facts About the Warriors

  • In the 2015–16 season, the Warriors finished with a 73–9 record, the best regular-season record in NBA history.
  • Stephen Curry became the first player in NBA history to be named unanimous MVP in 2016 and is widely regarded as the greatest shooter of all time.
  • In 2016–17, the Warriors had four All-Stars in the same season: Curry, Kevin Durant, Klay Thompson, and Draymond Green.
  • The 1975 Warriors championship over the Washington Bullets is often considered one of the biggest upsets in NBA Finals history.
  • The team’s colors—blue and gold—reflect both the California state flag and the Golden Gate Bridge.
